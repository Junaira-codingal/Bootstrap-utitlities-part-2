D.B. Cooper

The Man Who Stole $200,000 and Vanished Into Thin Air

On November 24, 1971, a man calling himself Dan Cooper boarded Flight 305 from Portland to Seattle. Mid-flight, he handed a note to a flight attendant, claiming he had a bomb. He demanded $200,000, four parachutes, and a fuel stop in Reno, Nevada.

The FBI met Cooper’s demands, and in Seattle, the passengers were freed. He then ordered the plane back into the air with only the pilots and a flight attendant. Somewhere over Washington state, at 10,000 feet, he jumped into the stormy night—and was never seen again.

Authorities launched one of the longest-running FBI investigations in history. Despite extensive searches, no parachute, body, or confirmed trace of Cooper was ever found. Only $5,800 of the ransom money surfaced in 1980, buried near the Columbia River, deepening the mystery.

For decades, theories have ranged from Cooper dying in the jump to him living a new life under a different identity. The FBI officially closed the case in 2016, but Cooper remains a legend—an unknown mastermind who pulled off the perfect heist and disappeared forever.
