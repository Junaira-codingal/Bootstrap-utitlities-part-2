Jack the Ripper

The Infamous Serial Killer

Jack the Ripper is the nickname given to an unidentified serial killer active in the late 19th century, specifically in the Whitechapel district of London. The killer's identity remains a mystery, and the case continues to intrigue both professional investigators.

The name "Jack the Ripper" was first used in a letter sent to the police in 1888, although there is no proof that the letter was written by the killer. The crimes involved gruesome murders, and the killer is believed to have been a male with knowledge of human anatomy, as the victims were mutilated in disturbing ways.

The victims of Jack the Ripper were primarily women, and the police were never able to catch the culprit, despite various investigations and theories about his identity. The killings stopped abruptly, further deepening the mystery.

Over the years, many theories and suspects have emerged, but none have been definitively proven to be the killer. The case remains one of the most famous unsolved mysteries in history.
